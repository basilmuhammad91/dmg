Goro cobalt and nickel mine, New Caledonia

Supporting the development of an open-pit mine in the municipality of Yaté designed to process mineral resources.

The Vale Nouvelle-Calédonie open-pit mine in the southern part of Grande Terre constitutes one of New Caledonia’s largest laterite ore deposits.

Following initial development of the mine, VINCI Construction Dom-Tom began further work in 2013 to build a 60-metre tailings pile (for materials with no nickel content) with a storage capacity of 20,000 m3 per day. The company adapted to the mining sector constraints of continuous 24/7 operation under all weather conditions. The large-scale project employed 160 people at peak activity.

Jesselton Résidences, Kota Kinabalu, Malaysia

Following a first contract for an office building, VINCI Construction Grands Projets delivered a set of residential units on the Kota Kinabalu seafront in Borneo.

Berjaya Central Park, Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia

New twin towers now grace the Kuala Lumpur skyline. Following handover of the first Berjaya Central Park high-rise building, also called the Menara Bangkok Bank for the largest buyer in June 2015, the second was completed at the end of 2016.

Nuclear facilities, Hinkley Point, United Kingdom

To ensure energy independence, the British government decided to build two 1650 MW EPR (pressurised water) type nuclear reactors at Hinkley Point near Bristol in southwest England.
